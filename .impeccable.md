## Design Context

### Users

Mugraph wallet is currently aimed at crypto-native early adopters using Cardano for private, instant payments. They are comfortable with wallet concepts, network context, addresses, and transaction flows, but still need the interface to feel fast and legible during real payment tasks.

### Brand Personality

Warm, approachable, and trustworthy. The product should feel confident without becoming cold, flashy, or over-engineered. Privacy and speed should read as calm competence, not hype.

### Aesthetic Direction

Design for a crypto product that does **not** look like a generic crypto dashboard. Keep the interface grounded and product-focused, with welcoming structure, clear hierarchy, and restrained surfaces instead of neon accents, dense glassmorphism, or ornamental Web3 tropes.

### Design Principles

1. Lead with clarity: primary actions and current state should be obvious within seconds.
2. Use space as hierarchy: tight grouping for related controls, generous separation between sections.
3. Make advanced crypto flows feel approachable: preserve technical detail without making the UI intimidating.
4. Prefer structured, useful surfaces over decorative cards and dashboard sameness.
5. Keep the mood warm and trustworthy, never loud or gimmicky.
